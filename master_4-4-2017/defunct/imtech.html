Integrated Mtech

The iM.Tech course curriculum consists of Core Courses and Electives spanning ten semesters.

While the same set of core courses have to be completed by all the students every semester, the students are free to choose their electives. While core courses help in creating a strong foundation in the Information Technology field, electives allow students to learn and specialize in their own areas of interest.

The core courses, categorized into Computer Science, Electronics and Communication, Basic Sciences and Miscellaneous entirely make up the first five semesters. Electives are also categorized into Computer Science, Electronics & Communication, Data Science, Networks & Communication. along with other core courses, are taken up from sixth semester onwards.

Though the electives of students may vary as per their interests, the number of electives per semester remains the same. The current semester requires students to take the same three core courses and any three electives.

    Computer Science

    • Event Extraction and Query System
      An application that extract events from the newswire text and using natural language processing and supervised learning techniques, adds them to a graph database and provides a system to query these events.
       
    • Job Scheduling In Cloud Considering Off-PeakPeriods
      The goal is to predict the future load on the system based on historical data using the auto-regressive integrated moving average (ARIMA) statistical model. Using these predictions, we decide whether a given time period is a peak period or an off-peak period. Finally, we propose a scheduling algorithm that schedules the jobs keeping in mind the peak and off-peak periods.
       
    • Animation in OpenGL using SceneGraph
      Designed and rendered a complex scene in C++ using the OpenGL library, utilizing scene-graph and MVC concepts. The scene had multiple independent and interdependent objects, various types of stationary and mobile light sources, optional textures and a GUI to let the user control various parts of the animation.
       
    • Speech Guided Robots in Cyber-Physical Environment
      Human-Robot interaction through speech through the use of phone based isolated speech recognition for a small vocabulary.
       
    • Sample tracker app for Neuro pathology lab at NIMHANS
      An Android application developed as per the requirements given by NIMHANS for tracking lab samples.
       

    Data Science

    • Deep Video Summarization Using CNN and bi-directional LSTM
      The main task is to summarize a given video by including only the key frames of the video. A model was trained using Convolutional Neural Networks integrated with a bi-directional LSTM with KL divergence as the cost function to take into account the temporal information between the frames as well.
       
    • Early identification of successful learners in MOOCs
      Designed a learning algorithm that given a student’s clickstream data, predicts the likelihood of him/her successfully completing the course. This algorithm was tested with OpenEdX data from Stanford University.
       
    • Allen AI Science Challenge (Kaggle challenge)
      A kaggle challenge to build a model that can answer multiple choice questions of 8th grade Science. A combination of several models was built which included an Information Retrieval System, statistical models with measures like mutual information and semantic similarity between words, and a network which analyses the text semantically and predicts the answer.
       
    • Convolutional Neural Networks
      Designed a CNN (state-of-the-art image processing technique) from scratch in MATLAB to perform traffic sign recognition.
       
    • Intelligent Restaurant Recommender Platform
      Mining dish based ratings from their respective restaurant reviews on social platforms like zomato and furthermore providing user customized recommendations on must try restaurants. Used - NLTK and Spacy Library in Python
       
    • Stock Price Prediction Model
      Built a hybrid stock prediction model to predict the movement of stocks traded on the Indian Stock Exchange. The internal motions of the stock market are modeled using linear multivariate regression and techniques with daily prices as time -series data. Additionally, inter-stock relations are found by clustering.
       
    • Personalised Web Search:
      A challenge to personalize the web search using the history of users' search behaviour with clicks and dwell time. A model was built which used the results from the traditional retrieval using Page Rank and reranked the pages based on Topic-Sensitive Page-Rank(TSPR) values given a query and a user.

  • Computer Science

    Web Sciences Lab

    The Web Science Lab (WSL) at IIIT Bangalore focuses on building models to extract semantics and understand the impact of the web on different facets of human life.
    Typical research pursuits of WSL include the following:

    • Mining semantic associations from text and social media data


    • Building computational models for various socio-cognitive phenomena on the web (like formation of collective opinions, creation of celebrities, etc.)


    • Semantic integration of formal web data


    Computational Sciences Lab

    The Computational Sciences Lab at IIIT-B is interested broadly in the areas of Algorithms, Optimization, and Robotics.

    Major focus areas include:

    • Robust optimization under uncertainty, with applications to supply chains, real time search, banking, smart grid, transportation, gaming and allied areas


    • Approximation algorims, machine learning, cryptography and linguistics, Visualization (scientific and information), high performance computing, computational geometry and topology



    • Electronic Design Automation including Statistical timing analysis and Optimization for Digital circuits, Power Analysis and Optimization, Formal Verification, Semiconductor manufacturing, Statistical Optimization, Combinatorial Optimization, Design and Analysis of Alogrithms



    Electronics & Communication

    HiDes Lab

    The High Density Electronic Systems Lab at IIIT-B focuses on research and development in the area of high density and low dimensional electronics. The areas of research include 3D electronics, magnetic logic devices, interconnects, and antennas.

    CEEMS Lab

    The Center for Electronics and Embedded Systems (CEEMS) Lab's objective is to nurture talent by focusing on Embedded Computing, Wireless Communication and Computer Vision. CEEMS Lab collaborates with public and private organizations with the aim of bridging the gap between academic output and industry requirements, thus providing every learner an equal opportunity to become industry ready.

    Networking & Signal Processing

    Multi Modal Perception Lab

    The Multimodal Perception lab focuses on human-centered sensing and multimodal signal processing methods to observe, measure, and model human behavior. These methods are used in applications that facilitate behavioral training, and social media analysis; and enable human-robot interactions (HRI). The focus is mainly on vision and audio modalities. Probabilistic graphical models from the backbone of the underlying formalism.

    Wireless Network Lab

    This lab focuses on research involving networking standards and technology. Current research work concentrates upon the latest broadband wireless access technology – WiMAX/LTE. Major areas of work include medium access control (MAC), QoS, QoE, power management, media independent handover (MIH).
