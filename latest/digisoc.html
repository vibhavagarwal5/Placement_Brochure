M.Sc in Digital Society

Recognizing the socio-economic potential of ICTs, and the increasing need to train professionals in harnessing this potential, IIIT-B offers a unique and innovative two-year Masters of Science Program in unpacking the dynamics of Digital Society. This interdisciplinary program will bring together diverse disciplines such as social sciences, humanities and the IT industry. Through this course, we aim to create a group of professionals who will: :

  • Contribute to the design, development and deployment of digital technologies for diverse social groups.
  • Provide and manage solutions that address persistent developmental challenges in domains such as education, governance, and health.
  • Engage in policy making and in advocacy for deploying digital technologies.
  • Enrich interdisciplinary research on the digital society.

Course Specifications

The course includes subjects which enable you to understand ICT and its deployment from multiple angles:

Interface design encourages you to think about designing technology for a diverse population. It enlists your brain to think about different types of users who interact with your design. It also aims to promote intuitive designing.Tracing artifacts back in time allows us to think about how, when and where the idea originated from. It creates the scope for examining how the artifact came to be what it is today. The digital divide has a large amount of scope for research.

To help students pursue this, we incorporate qualitative and quantitative research methodologies into the curriculum. Examining technology and understanding it from different standpoints is necessary to understanding the digital divide. There are courses which help you understand the policy implications of ICT. This course also provides you with the understanding of large scale digital systems. This allows students to understand how Request for Proposals for large scale projects come into being. There are a wide array of electives offered which allows students to dwell deeper into different areas like research, smart city solution deployments and product design.

Structured technique like using the MIT guidelines were employed for these projects.

QUALITATIVE RESEARCH

  • Field work was undertaken to understand the HR retention policies adopted by different startups in Bangalore. Sampling methodologies used were convenience, and snow ball. Typically, the partners and the founding owners were interviewed and an analysis report was prepared.
  • Field work was undertaken to understand the internet usage patterns among travelers/non-travelers in Coimbatore and Tiruppur using public WI-FI and mobile data. Tools used were structured interviews, participant observation, transcription, and coding with observation analysis.
  • Field work was undertaken to analysis the self-assessment of mid/senior level leaders in IT companies with respect to their Digital Readiness.

QUANTITATIVE RESEARCH

  • Statistical analysis was done to understand the trends in medical tourism in India. The focus was on International tourists coming to India for medical treatment. A survey and face to face interview was conducted among 12 doctors in various hospitals in Chennai who treat the medical tourists.
  • Statistical analysis with the field work was done to understand the Forms of payments (Online vs. Offline), preferred by consumers for utility services taking electricity as the domain with BSES Yamuna Power, New Delhi and BESCOM Bangalore as the chosen fields for research.
  • Statistical analysis to understand if there is a relationship between gender and privacy concerns and behaviour on Facebook among students of IIIT-Bangalore. Primary data was collected through closed-ended questionnaires distributed through Google forms among the students of IIIT-B.
